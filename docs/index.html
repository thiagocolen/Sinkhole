What is Sinkhole?

Sinkhole is an open-source Android application designed to provide a seamless and secure synchronization bridge between your local device storage and Google Drive. It was built for users who want total control over their data without relying on proprietary third-party sync services.

Whether you're managing personal documents, backup files, or an entire digital knowledge base, Sinkhole ensures your data is always mirrored and accessible across all your devices.

The Obsidian Use Case

Many users love Obsidian for their personal knowledge management, but syncing your vault between Android and Desktop can be challenging without paying for the premium Obsidian Sync service.

Sinkhole serves as the perfect, free alternative to Obsidian Sync:

  • Android Integration: Use Sinkhole to mirror your local vault folder on Android to a folder on Google Drive.
  • SAF Permission: Uses Android's Storage Access Framework for secure, granular access to your vault directory.
  • Desktop Sync: Access your Google Drive vault from your computer using the official Google Drive desktop app.
  • Unified Vault: Experience a single, unified vault across mobile and desktop for free.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Install the APK: Download the latest release from GitHub and install it on your Android device.
  2. Select a local folder: Open the app and select the folder you want to sync (e.g., your Obsidian vault). The app will request SAF permission.
  3. Authenticate with Google: Securely log in using your Google account via OAuth 2.0.
  4. Perform initial sync: Trigger the first synchronization. Sinkhole will identify changes using its manifest-based tracking system.
  5. Access on Desktop: Install Google Drive for Desktop on your computer. Your synced files will now be available locally for use with Obsidian or any other tool.
